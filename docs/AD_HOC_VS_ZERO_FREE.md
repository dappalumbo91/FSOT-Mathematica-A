# Ad hoc vs zero free parameters (FSOT)

## What “ad hoc” is for

Ad hoc parameters exist when a system must **hit a target without knowing the lawful direction** of the medium. You add knobs so technology **works for us** in a local environment.

| | Ad hoc | Zero free parameter (FSOT) |
|--|--------|----------------------------|
| Direction | Unknown → fit until it works | Seeds + folds fix direction |
| Purpose | Meet a goal under incomplete theory | Theory *is* the goal surface |
| Human role | Invent controls so tools serve us | Discover/allocate occupation in the fluid |
| Technology | Constructed emergence (engineered complexity) | Same medium; solidification into structure |

So yes: **there is a purpose to ad hoc** — it is the signature of **steering without full law**. It is not “evil math”; it is what humans do when building interfaces into a world they do not fully formalize.

## FSOT stance (not denial of technology)

- Technology does not “grow on trees”; it is **created**. That creation is still inside the fluid medium (as above, so below).
- Free parameters appear when we **force** a local fit instead of reading domain occupation (`D_eff`, observation, seeds).
- FSOT does not forbid tools. It forbids **pretending knobs are fundamental** when the spine is seed-derived.

## Scientific continuity (not replacement of literature)

FSOT may derive a unifying spine, but **the path here is already explained in literature** — Newton, relativity, the Standard Model, and related work. Deeper understanding still comes from those regimes.

Newtonian physics was **not outright replaced** by relativity; relativity recovers limits and deepens structure. Same pattern: **FSOT unifies; prior theories still teach.**
